Angelique Kerber is the sport’s quintessential counterpuncher, one who has spent the last decade-plus on tour effortlessly absorbing and redirecting the pace of her heavy-hitting rivals. It’s no surprise, then, that the practice court—not even the gorgeously scenic ones on offer at the Indian Wells Tennis Garden—offers little inspiration for a new mom and three-time major champion who feeds off the intensity of the match environment. “I practiced now for months,” Angelique Kerber sighed after reaching the fourth round of the BNP Paribas Open, which was the best result since her comeback from maternity leave began in January.
