It’s now or never for much of the U.S. men’s contingent. After Miami, the tour shifts quickly to European red clay, and stays there for two months. That’s the place and the surface where promising American seasons traditionally go to die, so the time to make hay is now. The upside is that the European players start thinking about heading home in Miami, and they’ve been known to tap out if they fall behind. Which Americans might be well-placed to take advantage of that? For example, Shelton’s path takes him through Lorenzo Musetti and on to Alcaraz, and many others..
