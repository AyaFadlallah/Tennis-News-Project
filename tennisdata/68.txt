Italian tennis player Andrea Rita has been suspended for 15 months as part of a corruption investigation, the International Tennis Integrity Agency said Friday. The ITIA said the Italian tennis player Andrea Rita, aged 23, admitted to four breaches of anti-corruption rules "including wagering on tennis matches and the failure to report a corrupt approach." The breaches took place from 2018-20. Rita was also fined 5,000 dollars, of which 4,000 dollars was suspended. Rita reached a career-high ITF singles ranking of 1,712 last June. The ITIA said that Andrea Rita cooperated fully with the investigation and accepted an agreed sanction.
