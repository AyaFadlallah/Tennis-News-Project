When Rafael Nadal reluctantly withdrew from Indian Wells, he tweeted, “Everyone knows how much I love this place and how much I love playing here.” 2013 was a year that best exemplified what the tournament has meant to him. After spending 222 days away from the tour due to a knee injury, Nadal had returned the previous month for the South American clay-court swing. He won two titles in three events there, but moving to hard courts would be a different entirely. It had been unforgiving surfaces that had contributed the most to his knee troubles in the first place.
